God is not beyond questioning

Written by : Olanrewaju Ezekiel

May 21 2023

Photo from unsplash

As Christians, we can find comfort in knowing that it is perfectly acceptable to seek understanding and ask God our heartfelt questions. In the realm of faith, there may be songs or phrases that suggest God is unquestionable, but it is important to recognize that our relationship with Him is built on a foundation of open dialogue and seeking truth.

Throughout history, people of faith have questioned God. In the book of Matthew 27:46, even Jesus Himself, in a moment of profound anguish, cried out to God, asking why He felt forsaken. This shows us that questioning is a natural part of our spiritual journey.

We must remember that God is not insecure or intimidated by our questions. Rather, He welcomes our sincere inquiries as a means for growth and deepening our relationship with Him.

Just as Job questioned God and received answers, we too can approach Him with our doubts, concerns, and yearning for understanding. When we encounter mysteries or face challenges that seem beyond our comprehension, it is crucial not to withdraw or suppress our feelings. Instead, let us come boldly to the throne of grace, as Hebrews 4:16 encourages us, understanding that boldness entails humility, honesty, and a genuine desire to seek God's wisdom.

The Scriptures remind us that we can present our case before God and reason with Him (Isaiah 1:18). This implies that He invites us into a dialogue, a partnership where our questions are valued and our perspectives are considered. God's ways are indeed mysterious, and there may be occasions when we struggle to grasp His plan or understand the reasons behind certain circumstances. However, when we draw closer to Him, remaining authentic and seeking answers, we cultivate a deeper trust in His sovereignty and purpose.

Our faith should never discourage us from asking questions or expressing our true emotions. Rather, it should encourage us to pursue a genuine, intimate relationship with our Heavenly Father, knowing that He desires to guide us, provide us with insight, and reveal His truth in ways that are uniquely meaningful to us.

God's willingness to be questioned and engage in sincere dialogue with His children is evident throughout the Bible. One such example is with Moses and the Israelites. When the Israelites offended God, He was prepared to destroy them and raise a new generation to enter the Promised Land. However, Moses interceded on their behalf. In Exodus 32:32, Moses pleaded with God, saying, "But now, if you will only forgive their sinâ€”but if not, please blot me out of your book that you have written. " God recognized Moses' sincerity and chose to relent from His initial decision.

In today's generation, many of us may hesitate to question God with such boldness and conviction. Yet, the story of Moses and the Israelites reminds us of the power of sincere intercession and seeking understanding from God.

It is crucial to understand why some Christians may struggle to see results in their lives. God's Word reminds us in Hosea 4:6, "My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge. " It is important to pay attention to the word "People" used in this context. God desires His children to seek knowledge and understanding, for it is through that pursuit that we can experience true freedom and abundant life.

Another story often used to defend the idea of unquestioning faith is that of John the Baptist.

However, it is essential to clarify that John's questioning was not done out of ignorance or a desire for answers. He was offended and intentionally used his words to provoke a reaction from Christ. While John the Baptist believed wholeheartedly that Jesus was the Messiah, intentionally provoking Him was not a wise choice. God examines the heart, and in this instance, He did not banish John, but rather affirmed his greatness while also lowering his rank in heaven (Matthew 11:11).

When faced with setbacks and failures, like a business venture that crumbles despite seeking God's guidance, we should not hesitate to approach Him with our questions. In fact, going back to God to seek understanding is not a sin. God may use those failures to teach us valuable lessons for the future. If we distance ourselves from Him and fail to inquire, we risk doubting His words and missing out on the wisdom and growth He wants to impart.

Let us never shy away from seeking understanding from God. Through honest questioning and sincere dialogue, we can deepen our faith and discover the depths of His love and wisdom. Proverbs 2:3-5 reminds us, "Cry out for insight and understanding. Search for them as you would for lost money or hidden treasure. Then you will understand what it means to fear the Lord, and you will gain knowledge of God."

In our relationship with God, we must remember that He desires a father-child connection with us. Just as we can question our earthly fathers with love and sincerity, God welcomes our questions. Our earthly fathers, who are mere mortals, do not take offense when we inquire about their decisions. How much more should we feel confident in approaching our heavenly Father, who created us and knows the depths of our hearts? God's ultimate desire is not for His children to fear Him in a paralyzing way, even though He is a God to be revered. Rather, He longs for us to approach Him without a trace of fear, knowing that He loves us unconditionally and wants to have open and honest conversations with us.
