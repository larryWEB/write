Tips for Securing Your First Freelancing Gig

Written by : Olanrewaju Ezekiel

May 21 2023

Photo by Olanrewaju on behance

Have you dedicated months, or even years, to mastering a skill, putting in all the hard work and effort, only to find yourself unrewarded for your endeavors? Are you feeling exhausted and frustrated, perhaps even on the verge of giving up? It can be incredibly disheartening to witness individuals who are less skilled or talented than you landing lucrative gigs. That feeling is truly demoralizing.

However, take solace in the fact that you are not alone. I have personally experienced the challenges you're facing.

During the stage you find yourself in now, I employed a set of effective marketing funnel strategies that propelled me from one project to the next with remarkable success. In fact, I was so busy that I had limited time for further learning.

In marketing, there are four crucial funnel stages that I followed:



A. The Awareness Stage:


Sitting back and waiting for job opportunities to come knocking at your door won't get you far. It is vital to create a buzz around your skills and make your presence known to as many individuals and companies as possible. Ensure that people near and far are familiar with your capabilities. Showcasing and promoting your previous projects that exemplify your skills is key.

Fortunately, we live in an era of social media, where you can leverage various platforms to share snippets of your work. The ultimate goal of this stage is to reach a point where you no longer need to introduce or explain what you do because your reputation precedes you.



B. The Consideration Stage:


During this stage, it is crucial to educate others and provide comprehensive information about your skills. Numerous potential clients may benefit from your expertise, yet they remain unaware of it. Therefore, it is essential to enlighten them.

Highlight the benefits of your service or skills and explain how they can contribute to their growth or profitability, depending on what you're offering.



C. The Conversion Stage:


This stage focuses on motivating potential clients to take action and hire your services. Avoid appearing desperate or overly pushy, as it can send the wrong message. Here's how to succeed in this stage:

Offer Discounts: Present your services at a discounted price. For instance, if your regular rate is $1,000, consider offering a 40% discount. A compelling offer of high-quality service at an affordable price is hard to resist.

Establish Deadlines: Creating a sense of urgency by setting time limitations for your offers will motivate potential clients to act promptly and secure your services.



D. The Loyalty Stage:


Once you've secured a client and completed a project, it's essential to maintain a strong and positive relationship. This will greatly benefit you in the long run. Fostering good relationships with your clients increases the likelihood of recommendations to their friends and family. Additionally, you'll be more likely to receive positive reviews, as you certainly wouldn't want a negative one.

Always go the extra mile to maintain a good relationship with your clients.

In conclusion, this strategy has worked wonders for me and my close friends whom I've shared it with. If you diligently apply the techniques outlined in this article, I'm confident that you'll be overwhelmed with paid gig opportunities within just a months.
